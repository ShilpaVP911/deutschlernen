Lesson 0001: The "Verb-Kick" Rule

In German, when you use the conjunction dass (meaning "that"), you are starting a subordinate clause. The most important thing to remember for B1 is that the verb moves to the very end of the clause.

The Rule: In a normal main clause, the verb is in the 2nd position. In a dass clause, the conjugated verb is "kicked" to the last position.

Examples

  • Main: Ich komme heute.
  • Subordinate: Ich weiß, dass ich heute komme.
